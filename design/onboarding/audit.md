## Persona walkthroughs

### (a) Grailed power user, referred by a friend

**Smiles:** Cover screen nails it in 5 seconds — "GRAILED · THE REALREAL · SSENSE — FILTERED TO YOUR SIZE" is exactly his pitch. The deck delights him; he swipes fast, milestone reads ("you keep pulling volume") feel like being seen. The Read screen is the peak — a spec sheet of his own taste is screenshot-bait he'll send to the group chat.

**Stalls:** Card 8 — he *knows* these pieces. No brand shown, but he recognizes the Kapital ring coat instantly, and the "pure visual signal" premise quietly leaks for exactly the user segment most likely to onboard first. Then the Read shows "BRANDS WE'D BET ON: Lemaire, Our Legacy" and he's a Yohji/CCP guy — inferred from 15 loves through attribute centroids, the brand guesses will be genre-adjacent but wrong, and he judges the whole model by that row. He hunts for the `+ add a name` input, which is deliberately tiny. He also asks the question no doc answers: **"where did the 15 pieces I pulled go?"** They vanish. Some don't even come in his size, because the deck wasn't size-filtered.

**Bails:** He doesn't — sunk cost carries him through signup. But if he swipes up to GRAIL on card 3 (voice doc taught him to) and the deck is the flow doc's binary build, the gesture dead-ends. And his biggest asset — hundreds of Grailed favorites — is never asked for; the buckets want camera-roll photos, not the structured taste history he's spent years building.

### (b) Curious, impatient normal shopper

**Smiles:** "NO FORMS. YOU JUST DECIDE THINGS." lands. First 15 swipes are genuinely fun; the FAINT→FORMING meter tick at card 10 is a good dopamine loop.

**Stalls:** If taste-science wins and the deck is 60 cards, she's out around card 25 — 60 swipes at real-world cadence (image loads on cellular, 3–4s per card, nobody preloads in any doc) is 3–4 minutes of one repeated action, not the promised "game." Jargon tax accumulates: SPECIMEN, REGISTRY, FIT LOCK, HUNT RANGE — she doesn't know what "HUNT RANGE" means and there's no `$` glyph until she reads the chips. At Fit Lock she's fine (25s is honest). At "claim my model" she'll do it if it's fast.

**Bails:** Screen 5, if she skips uploads — and she will, because photographing her closet is the definition of a task. She's now staring at a mono log ticker for **2+ minutes** with nothing to do. She backgrounds Safari; iOS may kill the page; she comes back to an ambiguous state. That ticker is the single most likely bail point in the whole flow for the mainstream user. Also: if Supabase email confirmation is ON (the tech doc's stated default), the build can't be claimed until she leaves the app, opens Mail, and taps a link. That's not a stall, that's an exit.

### (c) Email-hating skeptic

**Smiles:** Genuinely disarmed that there's no gate for 3+ minutes. Skip affordances being visible-but-quiet reads as respect. "the model is watching how you decide, not what you can afford" builds trust.

**Stalls:** Uploads — photographing the inside of his closet for an app he met 4 minutes ago is a privacy flinch, and no doc contains one line about where photos go, who sees them, or whether he can delete them. He skips all four buckets.

**Bails:** Screen 4. Email **and** password, mandatory, before seeing a single feed item. Sunk cost is real but he's precisely the persona it works least on. There is no Sign in with Apple — on an iOS PWA, the one auth method that lets him hide his email — and no "show me the feed once, then ask" fallback. If confirmation email is required on top, he's gone, and his 40 swipes of anonymous-session data become an orphaned row.

## Top 10 friction points / contradictions, ranked

**1. The catalog only contains Charles's sizes, so Fit Lock's promise is false for every user who isn't Charles.** The flow doc's proudest line — "nothing that doesn't fit will ever appear. not once." — is written against a catalog scrape-time-filtered to one body (`conductor.py in_size()`, TRR `["27","28","29"]`). User #2 with a 34 waist gets an empty or lying Pull 001 after 4 minutes of ceremony. The tech doc flags this as the only L-effort item; the flow and voice docs write copy as if it's done. **Fix:** the size-union scrape is a launch blocker, not a backlog item — sequence it before any non-Charles signup. Interim: Fit Lock checks entered sizes against actual catalog coverage and is honest on miss ("we don't stock your cut yet — first pull lands when we do") instead of shipping an empty feed behind an ironclad promise.

**2. Pull 001 is unpersonalized garbage under current code, and the fix isn't sequenced as a blocker.** `build_feed_cloud.py:226` gates stage-2 on `weights`, which don't exist until 300 taps; the prior-anchored fit is a design-doc proposal, not code. As written today, the entire onboarding produces a `base_score` feed identical for every user — the "model getting smarter about you" theater would be demonstrably fake on screen 6. **Fix:** the two-line stage-2 condition change plus the λ-blend prior (taste-science §4) are sprint-0, before any UI work. Add an acceptance test: two synthetic users with opposite deck swipes must get visibly different Pull 001s.

**3. The deck is 30, 40, or 60 cards depending on which doc you read, and the flow's exit ramp at 15 is below the science floor of 36.** Flow: 30 cards, ramp at 15. Voice: every string hardcodes 40 ("forty pieces", `SIGNAL 07/40`). Taste-science: floor 36, core 60, `prune_rare(min_df=8)` degenerates below ~36. A user taking the flow doc's card-15 ramp produces a fit that is literally bias + statement + formality. Pool sizes conflict too (120 / 240 / 80 across the three docs). **Fix:** converge on 40 — voice copy already written for it, above the 36 floor, inside the impatient persona's tolerance. Move the exit ramp to card 24 with honest copy ("ramp out now and the read stays rough"), and relocate taste-science's bonus rounds (+12) into the Screen 5 build wait — which also fixes point 8.

**4. Three-way contradiction on signup mechanics, with email confirmation set to kill the flow at its climax.** Flow: `signInAnonymously()` at Screen 0, `updateUser` conversion. Tech: no anonymous auth at all — localStorage buffer replayed after `signUp()`, and "keep confirmations ON, let the deck absorb the wait" — but in the flow's order the deck is *before* signup, so there is nothing to absorb the wait; the user hits a dead stop at peak momentum. Code reality: `index.html` has neither path. **Fix:** anonymous session is the source of truth (it gives a real `user_id`, so deck swipes satisfy the `signals` FK and RLS pre-signup; localStorage is offline fallback only). Turn email confirmation OFF for v1 or defer verification post-feed. Add Sign in with Apple — it's an iOS PWA and Apple's hide-my-email is the exact answer to persona (c). And gate the taste-row trigger / feed builds on non-anonymous users, or every drive-by visitor costs a vision-model build.

**5. Sizes have three placements and two incompatible widgets across the docs.** Flow: Screen 3, multi-select chips, after the Read. Taste-science: mid-deck interstitial between rounds 1–2. Voice: FITTING 04, after uploads, tap-to-cycle. Tap-to-cycle is single-value — a man who wears M and L, or 46 and 48 EU, cannot express that, and multi-size is core to the hard filter (`taste.payload.sizes` schema is plural everywhere). Also the flow's waist strip is `28…40` while the reference user's own scrape targets waist 27. **Fix:** flow's placement (post-Read, pre-signup — mid-deck kills swiping rhythm; post-upload is too late to gate the build) + flow's multi-select chips + voice's copy ("MEASUREMENTS", "close enough is fine"). Widen ranges to 26–44 with an overflow chip.

**6. The voice doc violates the flow doc's two foundational rules: no step counters, no blank fields.** `[ FITTING 01 / 06 ]` on every screen is exactly the "step 3 of 9" pattern the flow doc's first paragraph forbids, and it makes skips feel like skipping ahead in a numbered form. The HOUSES screen is a blank type-ahead asking users to recall brand names — form-energy the voice doc itself admits ("nothing is a blank field except…") — and it duplicates the Read's inferred-brand chips as a *separate screen*, resurrecting the survey. The signal ladders also disagree (BLIND→FAINT→FORMING→SET vs NONE→FAINT→FORMING→READING→LOCKED). **Fix:** keep "THE FITTING" as the ritual name, delete the `/06` numbering. Kill the HOUSES screen entirely; its copy ("compass points, never a fence", "LET THE DECK SPEAK") moves onto the flow's Screen 2 correction rows. Adopt one ladder: NONE → FAINT → FORMING → READING → SET, with SET reserved for the real 300-tap crossing.

**7. Nobody answers "where did my pulls go?" — and the unsize-filtered deck sets up a broken promise.** The user pulls 15 pieces, is told right-swipe means "pull it for me," then gets a Pull 001 containing none of them — and some *can't* appear because they don't come in his size, which collides head-on with Fit Lock's "not once" copy. The voice doc's PULL verb makes this worse by implying acquisition. **Fix:** one serif line on the Read ("the deck was calibration, not stock — you trained the eye, not the rack"), pin any in-size deck loves at the top of Pull 001 as `FROM YOUR FITTING`, and for out-of-size loves say it straight: "two of your pulls don't come in your size. the model kept the lesson."

**8. The 2-minute build wait has no path for upload-skippers — the mainstream bail point.** Screen 5's "zero perceived wait" claim is only true for users who upload; most won't on day one. A ticker is not an activity. iOS backgrounding during the wait risks a dead page with no documented resume. **Fix:** bonus swipe rounds during the build (absorbs taste-science's 60-swipe ambition without lengthening the pre-signup deck), progressive reveal of the top-scored items as stage-1 finishes ("first candidates surfacing…" with real thumbnails), and a resume-safe poll on `feeds.built_at` so backgrounding is harmless. Push notification on build-complete for users who add to home screen.

**9. Copy writes checks the system can't cash, and milestone reads will be wrong at n=10.** Voice promises: "grails are exempt — if the one appears, we'll show you anyway" (no exemption exists in any pipeline doc), "even one purchase teaches the model more than ten swipes" (science says 3x ≈ three swipes), "absorbed into your model overnight" (only if `onboard_brief.py` + a rebuild actually run nightly). And a card-10 read like "you pass on logos almost every time" fired from a 6/4 tally will be wrong often enough to break trust at the exact moment it's being built — especially for persona (a), who fact-checks. **Fix:** confidence-gate interstitial reads (fire only on ≥70/30 tallies, else neutral filler: "still triangulating. keep going."), delete the grail-exemption line until implemented, soften receipts copy to "money is the loudest signal there is" (already true, already written) and cut the "more than ten swipes" claim.

**10. Deck swipes will contaminate the model unless a source flag ships with the deck — and two docs quietly conflict on gestures and stamps.** Taste-science requires deck rows be flagged (`source:"deck"`) so `logprice`/`brand_rate` are dropped from features the user never saw; the tech doc's `persistSignal` reuses the existing upsert with no such flag, and `signals` has no source column. Unflagged, 40 deck rows teach price/brand noise forever. Adjacent gesture mess: flow says binary-only, taste-science and voice add GRAIL swipe-up, tech's stamps read `[ LOVED ]/[ PASS ]` in acid/*gold* — but gold is cart identity per UI-SPEC, and voice's stamps are PASS/PULL in ink/acid. **Fix:** push a `"deck"` tag into the existing `reasons` jsonb array (zero migration; `taste_model.py` filters on it when building feature dicts, and `build_deck.py` omits price/brand from `deck.json` entirely). Ship binary-only for the deck (flow is right: speed is the product; GRAIL returns in the feed where cart already exists), stamps PASS (ink) / PULL (acid), no gold anywhere in the deck.

## Two missed opportunities worth stealing back

- **Grailed favorites import for persona (a).** The power user's taste history already exists as structured data. Even v0 — "screenshot your Grailed favorites grid, we'll read it" — routes through the existing `extract_attrs` upload pipeline and is worth more than all four camera-roll buckets combined for that persona. Add it as a fifth intake path inside `[ THE RECEIPTS ]`.
- **One privacy sentence on the upload screen.** "your photos train your model and nothing else. private, deletable, never shown to anyone." Costs one serif italic line, converts the skeptic's biggest flinch, and every doc forgot it.
